- Conclusion:

  - Additional overhead exists beyond signals (result ring path, tuple encode/decode, Arrow/DataFusion path).
  - There is also a correctness mismatch that should be investigated in parallel with performance work.

- Interpretation from the probe:
  - The remaining budget is dominated by waiting for pages to move through the pipeline, not by raw signal delivery.
  - The two largest wait buckets are:
    - worker waiting for incoming page/message (`conn.poll().await`): `452 ms`
    - execution pipeline waiting for next heap page (`PgScanStream`): `478 ms`
  - Backend `WaitLatch` is real (`86.8 ms`) but smaller than worker-side starvation.
  - `tx.send(block).await`, result write, and result transport are tiny in this aggregate query.
  - Per-page transport itself is not huge:
    - backend->worker ~`50 us/page`
    - worker->backend request ~`25 us/page`
  - This points to lockstep pipeline starvation: worker and DataFusion repeatedly wait for the next page instead of staying fed, even though raw signals are cheap.
